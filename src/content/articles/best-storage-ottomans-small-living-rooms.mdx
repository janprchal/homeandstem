A storage ottoman is one of the few pieces of furniture that earns its floor space three
times over — extra seating, a soft coffee table surface, and hidden storage for blankets or
remotes. Here's what actually separates a flimsy cube from one worth keeping for years.

## What to Look for in a Storage Ottoman

### Weight capacity

If it needs to double as extra seating, check the rated weight capacity — basic folding
cubes top out lower than furniture-grade ottomans built on a real frame.

### Folding cube vs. built frame

Folding cubes are cheaper and pack flat for storage or moving. Built-frame ottomans (wood
legs, upholstered sides) look more like real furniture and last longer under daily use.

### Top surface usability

A flat, evenly padded top works as a coffee table for drinks or a laptop. A heavily tufted
or domed top looks nicer but is less practical as a surface.

### Material

Faux leather wipes clean easily and suits a casual room. Upholstered fabric (like waffle
weave or tufted linen) looks more like furniture but show stains more readily.
