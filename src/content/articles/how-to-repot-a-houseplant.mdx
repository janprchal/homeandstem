Repotting looks intimidating, but it's one of the easiest ways to keep a houseplant healthy.
This takes about **20 minutes**, costs around **$30** in supplies, and is firmly in
**beginner** territory.

<WhatYouNeedList variant="full" items={frontmatter.essentials} />

<StepBlock number={1} title="Water the plant a day before">
Moist roots slide out of the old pot more easily and suffer less transplant shock.
</StepBlock>

<StepBlock number={2} title="Ease the plant out">
Tip the pot and support the base of the stems. Gently squeeze a plastic pot or run a knife
around the edge of a rigid one.
</StepBlock>

<StepBlock number={3} title="Loosen the roots">
Tease apart circling roots with your fingers so they grow outward into the new soil.
</StepBlock>

<StepBlock number={4} title="Pot up and backfill">
Add fresh mix to the new pot, center the plant at the same depth as before, and backfill
around the sides. Water it in.
</StepBlock>

## Tips and Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Don't jump up several pot sizes — too much soil holds water and rots roots.
- Keep the plant at the same soil depth; burying the stem invites rot.
- Skip fertilizer for a few weeks while the roots settle.

<CalloutBox variant="warning" title="Watch the depth">
  Planting deeper than the original soil line is the most common repotting mistake. Keep the
  crown right at the surface.
</CalloutBox>
