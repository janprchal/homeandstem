If your indoor plants stretch, pale, or stall every winter, they're telling you they need
more light. A good grow light fixes that — but the specs can be baffling. Here's what
actually matters, and the lights we'd buy.

## What to Look for in a Grow Light

### Spectrum

Full-spectrum (white) LEDs cover the wavelengths most houseplants need and look pleasant in
a living room, unlike the old purple "blurple" panels.

### PPFD and coverage

Photosynthetic photon flux density tells you how much usable light reaches the leaves.
Match the fixture's coverage area to your plant grouping.

### Mounting and timer

Look for adjustable hanging height and a built-in timer so plants get a consistent
12–16 hour day.
