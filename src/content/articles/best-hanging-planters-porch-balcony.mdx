## What to Look for in a Hanging Planter

### Weight when wet

A hanging planter is much heavier full of wet soil than it looks empty — check your
bracket's or beam's weight rating before loading several at once.

### Self-watering vs. traditional

Self-watering reservoirs are worth the extra cost if you travel often or your porch gets
direct afternoon sun. Otherwise traditional macrame and wire baskets are cheaper and just as
attractive.

### Drainage and runoff

Coco-fiber liners and wire baskets drain freely — fine outdoors, messy over a wood deck.
Resin self-watering pots contain runoff better if drainage is a concern.

<CalloutBox variant="tip" title="Hanging tip">
  Stagger hanger lengths by 8–10 inches when grouping several plants — it reads as an
  intentional display instead of a row of identical pots at eye level.
</CalloutBox>
