A plant stand does two jobs at once: it lifts a plant into better light, and it turns a
single pot into an actual piece of room decor instead of something sitting on the floor.
Here's what separates a stand that wobbles under a heavy ceramic pot from one that doesn't —
and four we'd actually put in a living room.

## What to Look for in a Plant Stand

### Weight rating and material

Lightweight metal stands handle small-to-medium plastic or light ceramic pots well. A large,
fully-watered ceramic pot needs a stand explicitly rated for heavier loads, or solid wood
construction.

### Footprint and tier count

A single round stand needs almost no floor space. Multi-tier corner stands hold more plants
but need a genuine corner or wall gap to look intentional rather than cluttered.

### Indoor/outdoor coating

If the stand might ever move to a covered porch seasonally, a rust-resistant coated finish
matters. Purely indoor stands can skip this without issue.

### Stability with a top-heavy plant

Tall, top-heavy plants (a floor-height snake plant, a tree-form ficus) need a wider base or
a low center of gravity — check the stand's footprint relative to its height before buying.
